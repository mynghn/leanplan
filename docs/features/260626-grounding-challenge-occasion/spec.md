# 260626-grounding-challenge-occasion — Spec

## Behavior

### B-1: consultation-occasion-resolvable-from-one-home
From a single prose home, a reader can name the occasion on which a grounded rule's challenge consults the live context-engineering knowledge base — interrogating that rule's grounding (auditing, evolving, disputing, or authoring a new grounding) — and that the consultation is off the operational path (not during a stage run). One-shot verifiable: locate the home and confirm the occasion and its off-path nature are both stated there, without assembling them from scattered sections.

### B-2: two-challenge-events-distinguishable
A reader can distinguish the two events the framework calls "challenge": the implementation-time challenge (a committed artifact contradicts current reality → routes to `revise`) and the grounding challenge (a load-bearing rule's justification is questioned → consults the live source). One-shot verifiable: for each, a reader can state what triggers it and what it loads, without conflating them.

## Constraint

### C-1: minimal-altitude-no-new-machinery
The model is expressed as prose within existing homes — it introduces no new skill, stage, off-pipeline move, validator rule, or dedicated section-subsystem, and does not enumerate sub-occasions as formal concepts. Consultation stays modeled at the "on challenge, off the hot path" altitude. Continuously verifiable by inspection: the change adds prose, not framework surface.

## Non-goals
- The contract covers the consultation-occasion model and the "challenge" disambiguation only — not the live-resolution mechanism, the gloss fallback, or the reference-link freshness gate (each owned elsewhere).
